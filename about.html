About

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Being an island in the Pacific, weather conditions can always be a bit unpredictable. Ensure that you keep an eye on the weather to prepare for your day on the island as some locations may not give you a refund due to poor weather conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do about charging my phone/laptop while visiting?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

What is the policy on drinking?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is officially 18, but it is not strictly enforced.

Will it be easy to communicate while visiting Taniti?

Many younger TanitiansÂ speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

What happens if I get injured during my stay?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Do I need to be concerned about crime?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Are there days where locations close?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays. You and anyone in your party should plan accordingly.

What currency and payment methods are usable during my stay?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
